Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about Subvoquill and subvocal speech technology.

Subvocal speech, also known as silent speech, refers to the minute muscle activations in your throat and mouth that occur when you think about speaking or silently mouth words. Your brain sends neural signals to your vocal muscles even when no sound is produced. Subvoquill's EMG sensors detect these subtle electrical patterns and decode them into commands.
No. Subvocal speech produces no audible sound. Subvoquill captures only the electrical signals from your muscles — it's completely silent to anyone around you. Your silent commands remain private, secured with end-to-end AES-256 encryption between the device and your chosen agent.
Subvoquill achieves 97% accuracy after a brief personalization period (typically 10-15 minutes of training). The on-device neural network continuously adapts to your unique physiology, improving over time. Initial out-of-box accuracy is approximately 85%, which rapidly improves with regular use.
Yes. Subvoquill features adaptive noise cancellation that filters out motion artifacts in real-time. While extreme physical activity may reduce accuracy slightly, normal walking, sitting, and everyday movements have minimal impact on performance.
Privacy is foundational to Subvoquill. All neural decoding happens on-device — your raw EMG data never leaves the wearable. Only decoded text commands are transmitted over an encrypted BLE link. We use AES-256 end-to-end encryption, and no subvocal data is stored on our servers. You own your neural data, period.
Subvoquill uses an open agent protocol, meaning it can route commands to virtually any app or AI agent. Out of the box, we support ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Siri, Alexa, Home Assistant, Agent Zero, and LangChain-based agents. Our SDK allows developers to integrate any service that accepts text input.
